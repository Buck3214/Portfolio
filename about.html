How I got into programming


Through friends, I learned about a program that paid 250 dollars, and without hesitating, I took the offer. Later, I learned that it was a programming course, which at the time I defined as “nerd stuff”. Once there, The instructors gave a brief introduction of themselves and explained what coding is. Again, I learned something new; this course specifically focused on front-end development with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. After the first day, I was bewildered by the amount of information that was programming. Getting home, I immediately made my first website that featured a yellow background, a title, and bullet points filled with memes and pictures of my friends.
